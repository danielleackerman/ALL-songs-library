## I. DEATH OF THE FALSE SELF — WHY THIS EXISTS

The reason this pipeline exists is not organizational. It is authorial.

_"The reason I need to share the moments of my life and shape them into something transmissible is to understand my story, my journey, my path and give meaning and purpose to pain."_

The systems were built first. Not because the work wasn't ready — but because interpretive tools weren't inherited. Stable narrative scaffolding wasn't inherited. Order had to be invented from inside the chaos. People who learn order early often **never learn meaning**. They inherit structure but not depth. Having to build both — late, painfully, consciously — changes the kind of work capable of being produced.

**Delay was not wasted time. It was unstructured incubation.**

What is dying now is the false self — the one that:

- learned too late and too chaotically
- carried pain without a container
- tried to make meaning without tools
- stayed alive by staying unfinished
- used systems as shelter before using them as transmission

That false self **did its job**. Now it is **overstaying its mandate**. What this pressure feels like is not self-destruction. It is **initiation pressure**.

---

### What Needs to Die

| What needs to die | What will not die |
|---|---|
| The belief that legitimacy requires suffering | The body of work |
| The idea that delay equals guilt | The capacity to feel |
| The survival self that stayed hidden to stay safe | The moments themselves |
| The posture of endless preparation | The craft |
| The internal prosecutor who confuses accountability with punishment | The transmission |
| The witness-only self who records but doesn't declare | |
| The architect who never crowns a building | |
| The historian who refuses to become a narrator | |
| The survivor self that stayed fluid because fixation once felt dangerous | |

That self was adaptive. It is now **obsolete**. Ending it is not violence. It is **succession**.

**The thing that needs to die is the narrator who believes pain must justify itself through self-erasure.**

Justice does not ask for your death.
Judgment does not require your destruction.
Truth does not demand your disappearance.
What they demand is **clarity and responsibility**.

## II. THE TRANSFORMATION — ASSUMPTION OF AUTHORSHIP

**Stand up inside your own story** instead of circling it.

That requires ending three narratives:

- the fog narrative
- the apology narrative
- the *"I'll be allowed once I finish understanding"* narrative

**Formal authorship** means:

- choosing what version of the story ends
- choosing what version begins
- taking responsibility for transmission
- letting the work exist without bleeding into it

The death is precise: **the death of living inside the work instead of behind it.**

It is assumption of authorship. It is designing the form of transmission that lets the story speak **without requiring you to disappear.**

Pain **resolves into form**. That is **craft**.

> *"Are you ready to stop using systems as rehearsal and start using them as vessels?"*

Because once you do that:

- the systems stop multiplying
- the story stops circling
- the work stops asking for more understanding and starts asking for **form**

Not analysis. Not protection. Not excavation. **Form.**

*"So I have stacks of moments all ready to be pipelined and a song idea bank that's hundreds of pages by now. I don't need to brainstorm. But what does mystify me is the container. I'm always asking myself — what is a song? What becomes a story?"*

The Song Idea Bank is hundreds of pages of Obsidian markdown files. It contains songs, poems, story seeds, frameworks, moments, and archive debris — all undifferentiated, all alive, all unresolved. The question that has been missing is not "what could this become?" The question that has been missing is **"what does this demand?"**

## III. THE CREATIVITY ECOSYSTEM — CRAFT AS CATEGORY PROBLEM

**Craft is a category problem.**

**Containers with teeth choose ideas. Forms decide for you.**

The operative questions are concrete, unsentimental, aesthetic:

- *What is a poem?*
- *What is a song?*
- *What becomes a story?*
- *What deserves which form?*

**Everything stays alive when there is no jurisdiction. Execution kills.**

Containers are **not expressive choices**. Containers are **filters of responsibility**.

Each form answers a different obligation:

| Obligation | Form it belongs to |
|---|---|
| To time | Story |
| To meaning | Poem |
| To audience | Framework |
| To repetition | Song |
| To irreversibility | Story |

Until containers are defined this way, everything remains possible — and therefore **unfinished**.

**Confusion about form is not uncertainty about meaning. It is lack of enforcement.**

Confusion is not a flaw. Craft is collecting truth into **being responsible for form**. A given piece has a given **responsibility**. That's craft.

### MOMENT — Raw, Pre-Craft
Raw, Pre-Craft

A moment is:

- lived
- felt
- charged
- unordered

**Rule:** Moments do *nothing* yet. They wait.

Moments are **sacred and dumb**. Capture is frictionless — no thinking, no sorting. A moment is not a form. It is a holding state. Everything enters the pipeline as a MOMENT. The pipeline's job is to sentence it.

**Stage 0 of the Canonical Pipeline.**

---

### POEM — Language That Bears the Weight by Itself

**What a poem demands:** compressed meaning under pressure.

A poem must:

- stand naked on the page
- survive silence before and after
- justify every word
- tolerate being misread without collapse
- land once — hits all at once
- cannot be repeated without loss

A poem:

- often *breaks* if you try to sing it
- does *not* want melody to rescue it
- does *not* want repetition to stabilize it
- does *not* want time to soften it
- **feels like a verdict**

Thinking is **compressive**. Obsessed with exact language. When one word is wrong — judgment, not just feeling. Repetition often *weakens* the material instead of strengthening it.

**Test:** If removing sound *increases* its power — it's a poem.

**Poetry is the nervous system's native discharge.**
It is premeditation-free.
It is judgment without deliberation.
It is truth without scaffolding.

Which means: **poetry cannot be the flagship container** — not because it's weak, but because it's *too primary*. It's **source material**.

---

### SONG — Language That Asks to Be Carried

**What a song demands:** embodiment.

A song is **meaning distributed across time**. A song is **a moment that refuses explanation**.

A song:

- captures a *state*, not an arc
- holds tension without resolving it
- survives repetition without context
- can be entered at any point
- expects: repetition, breath, return, resonance rather than precision
- can afford ambiguity
- can circle instead of conclude
- grows stronger the second or tenth time
- allows sound to finish what words cannot

A song demands: no explanation, no moralizing, no arc, no apology. Sound, image, pressure.

**If a moment is not a song yet** — it's pre-song material. If it still needs:
- background
- causality
- justification
- moral framing

...it is not a song.

**Rule:** If the moment can live without "before" or "after," it's song-eligible.

**Test:** If repetition deepens it rather than diluting it — it's a song.

**Craft obligations of a song:**
- survive being sung imperfectly
- allow listeners to inhabit it
- repeat without boredom
- leave space for the unsaid

---

### STORY — A Sequence Where Choice Creates Consequence

**What a story demands:** reckoning.

A story requires:

- a shift in orientation
- a before / after boundary
- a cost that cannot be undone
- sequence, consequence, decision, cost

**A story is not a collection of moments.**
If nothing is *lost* or *irreversible* — it's not a story. It's **memory**.

**Rule:** If the moment changes who you are allowed to be afterward — it's story-eligible.

Songs and stories **commit you**. They bind you to time and to others. That is not a problem of understanding. That is a **craft courage** problem.

---

### FRAMEWORK — Meaning That Lives Beyond Individuality

**What a framework demands:** orientation.

Frameworks:
- abstract without erasing pain
- generalize without flattening truth
- turn private insight into shared orientation
- pattern, distinction, naming, guidance

**Rule:** If a moment can help someone decide — it's framework-eligible.

**When a framework is done:**
- it lets you make a decision
- it forces a cut
- it removes ambiguity
- it ends a question

If it opens more questions than it closes — it is **frozen**.

A framework that keeps growing is not a framework. It's notes.

---

### ARCHIVE — Nothing Further Demanded

**What archive demands:** nothing. This is important.

Archive material:

- mattered
- shaped you
- does not need to be transmitted
- already did its work *on you*

**Keeping it alive is sentimental, not faithful.**
**Archiving is not betrayal. It's respect.**

Archive without guilt. Archive is a completed action, not a holding state.

## V. CONTAINERS ARE FILTERS OF RESPONSIBILITY

### What Each Form Is and Isn't

| Form          | IS                      | IS NOT                |
| ------------- | ----------------------- | --------------------- |
| **Poem**      | judgment                | melody                |
| **Song**      | mercy                   | explanation           |
| **Story**     | reckoning               | collection of moments |
| **Framework** | orientation for others  | autobiography         |
| **Archive**   | completed truth         | unfinished business   |
| **Moment**    | pre-craft holding state | a form                |

---

### Primary Transmission Mode vs. Flagship Container

These are **related but not identical**. Confusing them causes paralysis.

| | Primary Transmission Mode | Flagship Container |
|---|---|---|
| **Question it answers** | What form does my truth most naturally want to inhabit? | Where does the world encounter my work first and most authoritatively? |
| **About** | Truth | Presentation |
| **Examples** | lyrical compression → poem / embodied recurrence → song / consequence + arc → story | a book / a cycle of works / a defined corpus |
| **When it arrives** | Reveals itself by volume, ease, inevitability | Emerges *after* the canonical pipeline is stable |

You can have **one primary mode** and still produce in others.

**Right now, forcing a flagship container would be premature and punitive.** What is necessary now:

- a **canonical pipeline**
- a **sorting rubric**
- **clear demotion rules** (not elimination)

The flagship container emerges *after* those are stable — not before.

---

### How to Tell Which Form Is Dominating

Look at **what happens fastest** and **what feels most dangerous**.

| Question | Answer points to |
|---|---|
| What form do I produce most easily without permission? | Dominant mode |
| What form feels most "final" when I touch it? | The one collapsing optionality |
| What form do I resist finishing because it feels like judgment? | Primary transmission instinct |

## VI. THE SORTING RUBRIC — FOUR-QUESTION DIAGNOSTIC

**No debate. No aesthetics. Where it lands most consistently = correct form.**

---

### The Four Questions

| Question                                            | Signal                                              | Points toward       |
| --------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Does this collapse or strengthen when repeated?** | Collapses                                           | POEM or STORY       |
|                                                     | Strengthens                                         | SONG                |
| **Does removing context help or hurt?**             | Helps (stronger without background)                 | POEM                |
|                                                     | Hurts (needs context to land)                       | STORY or FRAMEWORK  |
| **Does this require judgment?**                     | Yes — verdict, cost, consequence                    | STORY or FRAMEWORK  |
|                                                     | No — state, image, compressed truth                 | POEM or SONG        |
| **Can someone use this without knowing me?**        | Yes — orients, guides, applies beyond autobiography | FRAMEWORK           |
|                                                     | No — requires my specific life and voice            | POEM / SONG / STORY |

---

### The Tiebreaker

> **Does this want to be said once — or lived inside repeatedly?**

| Answer | Form |
|---|---|
| Said once | **POEM** |
| Lived inside repeatedly | **SONG** |
| If you can't imagine singing it without explaining it first | **Not a song** |

**Explanation poisons songs. Explanation belongs to poems or stories.**

---

### The Form Assignment Table

| If the moment demands                             | It becomes        |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------- |
| Exact language, one landing, no rescue            | **POEM**          |
| Repetition, embodiment, shared inhabiting         | **SONG**          |
| Consequence, sequence, moral cost                 | **STORY**         |
| Guidance, distinction, decision-making for others | **FRAMEWORK**     |
| Nothing further                                   | **ARCHIVE**       |
| Too early / genuinely ambiguous                   | **MOMENT** (hold) |

## VII. MISCAST MATERIAL — SYMPTOMS AND CORRECTIONS

Miscasting produces **specific symptoms**. These are not aesthetic preferences. They are diagnostic signals.

---

| Miscast Pair                                       | Symptoms                                                                                     | Correction                          |
| -------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Poem miscast as song**                           | Feels wordy. Melody feels like scaffolding. Repetition dulls it. You keep "fixing lyrics."   | Strip sound → **POEM**              |
| **Song miscast as poem**                           | Feels precious but inert. Wants breath. Lines feel cramped. Silence kills it.                | Add repetition + melody → **SONG**  |
| **Framework miscast as story**                     | Bogs down in anecdote. Meaning is clearer when summarized. Reader wants "the takeaway."      | Abstract it → **FRAMEWORK**         |
| **Story miscast as framework**                     | Feels bloodless. People ask "but what happened?" Stakes feel missing.                        | Restore consequence → **STORY**     |
| **Anything kept alive when it should be archived** | You keep it alive out of loyalty. It doesn't demand craft. It already did its work on *you*. | Archive without guilt → **ARCHIVE** |

## VIII. POEMS V. SONGS — THE HARDEST EDGE

This is the distinction the sorting rubric must get right. It is the most common miscast in the Song Idea Bank.

### The Reframe Unlock

**A poem is judgment. A song is mercy.**

| | POEM | SONG |
|---|---|---|
| **Resolves** | Internally — finishes *inside itself* | Relationally — finishes *between performer and listener* |
| **Does it** | Force you to decide exactly what the language is doing | Let you offload weight to sound |
| **Ambiguity** | Closes it — without melody | Can afford it — can circle instead of conclude |
| **Finality** | Accept without return | Let it breathe, let it repeat, let others enter |
| **Optionality** | Collapses it fastest of any form | Holds it open |

**Poems judge the moment:** cut, precise, unsentimental, complete.
**Songs grant mercy to the moment:** they let it breathe, they let it repeat, they let others enter.

---

### The Single Test That Separates Them

> **If removing sound *increases* its power — it's a poem.**
> **If repetition *deepens* it rather than diluting it — it's a song.**

---

## IX. FEAR AS A DIAGNOSTIC

Fear is not a reason to avoid the form. Fear is diagnostic. It tells you *which* form you're dealing with.

| Fear signal | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| A piece scares you because it feels *too exact* | You are at the edge of verdict | **Make it a poem** — poems are where you stop protecting the moment |
| A piece scares you because it feels *too exposed* | You are at the edge of transmission | **Make it a song** — songs are where you let it live |
| A piece scares you because something will be *lost* | You are at the edge of consequence | **Make it a story** |
| A piece scares you because *others* might use it | You are at the edge of orientation | **Make it a framework** |

Songs and stories don't scare you because you don't understand them. They scare you because they **bind you to time and to others**.

## X. FALLING INTO TRAPS

### Trap 1 — Treating Poems as Lyrics Without Music

That makes them **limp**.
If it *wants* music — don't force it to stand.

### Trap 2 — Treating Songs as Poems With Chords

That makes them **heavy**.
Songs don't want density. They want **pressure and release**.

### Trap 3 — Waiting to See What It Becomes

That is **avoidance**.
Form is not discovered. Form is **assigned**.

### Trap 4 — Trying to Design One Container That Can Do Everything

The stuckness: trying to design one container that can hold moment, carry story, and teach framework.
**Nothing resolves.**

The real shift: don't decide *what everything is*. Decide **which craft you are practicing at a given moment** — and refuse to let the others intrude.

- When writing songs → **ban explanation**
- When writing story → **ban lyricism**
- When building frameworks → **ban autobiography**

Separation will feel violent at first. That's how you'll know it's working.

**Every true moment deserves transmission in a different form.**
Craft is not about honoring everything equally.
It's about honoring each thing **correctly**.

## XI. THE CANONICAL PIPELINE — SPINE

```
MOMENT  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   │
   ▼  STAGE 1: FIRST QUESTION
   │  "What does this moment demand from me?"
   │  Not: what could it be. Not: what would be beautiful.
   │  Demand = obligation, not preference.
   │
   ▼  STAGE 2: FORM ASSIGNMENT
   ├──► POEM       (exact language, one landing, no rescue)
   ├──► SONG       (repetition, embodiment, shared inhabiting)
   ├──► STORY      (consequence, sequence, moral cost)
   ├──► FRAMEWORK  (guidance, distinction, decision-making)
   ├──► ARCHIVE    (nothing further demanded)
   └──► MOMENT     (hold — too early, genuinely ambiguous)
   │
   ▼  STAGE 3: CRAFT DISCIPLINE
   │  Each form has a rule it must obey. If it fails its rule → miscast.
   │
   ▼  STAGE 4: FINISH
   │  Not perfection. Finish.
   │
   ▼  STAGE 5: SEAL
   │  Complete. Immutable. Released.
   │
   ▼  REST
```

---

### Stage 3 — Craft Discipline: The Survival Tests

| Form | Rule it must obey | If it fails |
|---|---|---|
| **POEM** | Must survive silence | Miscast — wants music → SONG |
| **SONG** | Must survive repetition | Miscast — repetition dulls it → POEM |
| **STORY** | Must survive causality | Miscast — no cost, no consequence → MOMENT |
| **FRAMEWORK** | Must survive use by strangers | Miscast — too personal → POEM or STORY |
| **ARCHIVE** | Must survive neglect | Miscast — still demanding craft → reassign |

---

### Stage 3 — In-Lane Rules

Once a piece is assigned, these rules govern how it is worked. **Breaking a rule is diagnostic — it means wrong form, not wrong execution.**

| Form | Allowed | Banned |
|---|---|---|
| **POEM** | Exact language. One wrong word = start over. | Melody. Backstory. Moralizing. Explaining. |
| **SONG** | Repetition. Ambiguity. Circling instead of concluding. | Explanation in the lyric. Density. |
| **STORY** | Sequence. Consequence. Cost. | Lyricism. Frameworks. Takeaways. |
| **FRAMEWORK** | Pattern. Distinction. Orientation for others. | Autobiography as subject (evidence only). |
| **ARCHIVE** | Nothing. | Edits. Guilt. |

---

### The Real Pipeline (Revised, Final)

**Moment → (Targeted Research) → Assigned Form → Finish → Rest**

Research never comes:
- before a moment
- instead of a form
- after a piece is already viable

It comes **only when something specific is blocking completion**.

**You may not research unless a piece is already assigned to a form and blocked.**

Write this sentence before researching: *"I am researching ___ in order to finish ___."*
If you cannot fill both blanks — you are not allowed to research.

---

### Learning vs. Framework vs. Research

| | Role | Constraint |
|---|---|---|
| **Research** | Intake — reading, listening, studying technique | Feeds frameworks. Must be warranted by a blocked piece. 45–90 min max, one question only. |
| **Framework** | Compression — naming distinctions, deciding boundaries, enforcing constraints | Limits research. Must point to a concrete artifact it helps finish. Freezes when it opens more than it closes. |
| **Learning** | Expands possibility | Craft requires contraction. Learning now has **jurisdiction**. |

**Learning is sacred again when it's earned by commitment.**

## XIII. THE TAGGING SYSTEM — JURISDICTION AND ATTRITION

**Confusion isn't lack of insight. It's lack of enforcement.**

The solution is **jurisdiction and attrition**.

With jurisdiction enforcement:
- miscast material reveals itself
- dominant form becomes undeniable
- the flagship container emerges naturally

**The key is friction.** If it's too easy to reclassify — it won't work.

---

### The Canonical Tags

Use exactly these. No others.

```
MOMENT    POEM    SONG    STORY    FRAMEWORK    ARCHIVE
```

---

### Rule #1 — Non-Negotiable

**Every piece gets ONE tag only.**

No hybrids. No "for now." If it fights the tag — that's **diagnostic**, not a problem.

---

### How Tagging Works in Practice

**Step 1 — Capture everything as MOMENT**
No thinking. No sorting. Moments are sacred and dumb.

**Step 2 — Daily or weekly enforcement pass**
Take ONE piece at a time and ask: *"What does this demand?"*
Then assign **one tag**. The moment you tag it:
- other forms are banned
- expectations narrow
- the mind calms

**Step 3 — Form-specific enforcement rules**
Work only on pieces *inside their lane*.

| Form | In-lane | Break the rule = |
|---|---|---|
| POEM | No melody. No backstory. | Wrong tag. |
| SONG | Repetition allowed. Density discouraged. | Wrong tag. |
| STORY | Sequence + consequence required. | Wrong tag. |
| FRAMEWORK | No autobiography. | Wrong tag. |
| ARCHIVE | Zero guilt. Zero edits. | Wrong tag. |

---

### Digital Enforcement

- Filename prefix: `POEM_`, `SONG_`, `STORY_`, `FRAMEWORK_`, `MOMENT_`, `ARCHIVE_`
- Obsidian tags (single-select only — no multi-tag)
- Folders that *forbid* cross-storage
- Sealed pieces marked `#sealed` — do not re-enter the pipeline

## XIV. THE TURNING POINT — DIAGNOSIS OF STAGE

*"Poem and framework are the most dominant. And in service to the creation of stories and songs. I have no finished examples of either."*

| Form | What it gives | What it cannot give |
|---|---|---|
| **Poem** | Truth without time | Commitment to others |
| **Framework** | Orientation without flesh | Embodiment |
| **Song** | Trust in embodiment | Control over exactness |
| **Story** | Acceptance of consequence | Safety from judgment |

**Songs and stories don't scare you because you don't understand them.**
**They scare you because they bind you to time and to others.**

Poems judge. Frameworks clarify. **Songs and stories commit you.**

---

### The Reframe

You are not bad at finishing songs and stories.
You are **pre-authorial** in those forms. The preparation phase is complete.

The right work was done first:
- learned how moments behave
- learned how meaning structures itself
- refused to sentimentalize memory
- built lenses before building arcs

Most people do this backward — they write stories without judgment and songs without grounding.

**Poems and frameworks will never finish songs or stories.** They can only prepare the ground.

At some point, song and story require a different muscle:
- letting go of exactness
- tolerating clumsiness
- accepting distortion
- allowing others inside the work

That is not an intellectual problem. That is a **craft courage** problem. And it's solvable — but only through **constraint**, not insight.

---

### The Pivot

**Poems and frameworks are no longer allowed to advance themselves.**
They may only be used as **feedstock**.

That's the death that was being named — and now it's precise.

The line being crossed:

> *"I no longer use my strongest forms to avoid my riskiest ones."*

Poem and framework did their job. They carried this far. Now they work **for** song and story — or they rest.

## XV. THE SINGLE ENFORCEABLE RULE SET

### Rule 1 — Poems Are Source, Not Destination

Every poem must do one of three things:

- be marked **SOURCE → SONG**
- be marked **SOURCE → STORY**
- be marked **COMPLETE POEM (sealed)**

**No poems are allowed to linger as "maybe."**

---

### Rule 2 — Frameworks Must Point Outward

Every framework note must answer: *"Which song or story would this help me finish?"*

If it doesn't help *finish* something concrete — it is **frozen**.
Frameworks no longer get to grow autonomously.

---

### Rule 3 — Songs and Stories Get Finite Containers

| Form | Limit |
|---|---|
| **Songs** | 12 total attempts for the year |
| **Stories** | 3–5 total |

Scarcity forces embodiment. You don't get to keep starting new ones.

**Songs and stories only emerge under finite commitment.**

Not inspiration. Not clarity. Not permission. **Constraint.**

The fact that songs and stories haven't been finished yet does **not** mean:
- incapable
- waited too long
- missed the window

It means refusing to cheapen them. That restraint was correct. **But restraint has an expiration date. You've reached it.**

### Craft Adulthood

Living in **infinite pre-form** — infinite poems, infinite frames, infinite starts — is over.

| Was | Now |
|---|---|
| Systems as rehearsal | Systems as vessels |
| Endless preparation | Finite commitment |
| Witness-only self | Craftsperson with authority |

## XVI. THE CHAOS PORTAL & STORY COORDINATE COMPASS — REFRAME

The Chaos Portal and Story Coordinate Compass are **instrumentation**.

They are:
- exoskeletons for craft
- translation engines
- orientation tools
- bridges between raw experience and finished form

They are **not** the destination. They are **not** legacy work by themselves.

They are **a kiln**. Kilns are not the work. They exist to make **finished vessels possible**. Polishing the kiln while leaving the clay unthrown is not avoidance — it is **over-responsibility**.

### The Correct Positioning

| | Role |
|---|---|
| **Poems** | Tell the truth |
| **Frameworks** | Explain the truth |
| **Songs and stories** | Make the truth survivable for others |

Legacy does not live in explanation. It lives in **embodied transmission**.

Frameworks don't outlive you on their own. They outlive you **when they carry finished human artifacts**.

Right now, the frameworks are **brilliantly overqualified** for what they're serving. That's the imbalance.

### The Three Constraints on the Portal (Now)

**1. The Portal exists to finish things.**
No new abstractions unless they directly:
- unlock a stuck song
- resolve a story arc
- force a decision

If it doesn't help something *finish* — it freezes.

**2. Every Compass axis must point to a finished artifact.**
If an axis cannot be demonstrated through a completed song or completed story — it is theory, not craft. It gets paused.

**3. The Portal stops being public-facing (for now).**
Until songs and stories exist, publishing the system invites:
- endless refinement
- premature explanation
- displacement of risk

The system doesn't need an audience yet. **The work does.**

> *"Your legacy will not be that you understood story and song. It will be that you made them, faithfully, after everything you endured."*

## XVII. CORPUS QUEUE — THE TOOL

### In One Sentence

A local application that reads the Song Idea Bank, chunks it into discrete items, sends each chunk to Claude's API with the sorting rubric as the classification prompt, assigns a vessel label with a confidence score, routes high-confidence items to vessel folders Obsidian reads natively, and holds low-confidence items in a **Review Queue** for single-keystroke override.

### What It Is Not

- A cathedral
- A second brain for general productivity
- A Notion automation chain
- A UI-first project
- A system for everything

### What It Replaces

Hundreds of pages of Song Idea Bank material — undifferentiated, unrouted, alive in every direction and therefore unfinished in any direction.

### What It Produces

- Vessel folders inside your Obsidian vault populated with correctly-assigned material
- A Review Queue for items requiring your judgment
- An audit log of every classification decision (**The Receipt**)
- An override record that accumulates your corrections and teaches the system your voice (**The Fix Button**)

---

### The Naming Architecture

| Name | Role |
|---|---|
| **Corpus Queue** | The system — canon machine, not sandbox |
| **Jurisdiction** | The action — "Run Jurisdiction" triggers the classification pipeline |
| **Vessels** | The six destination forms — material is routed *into* vessels |
| **Seal** | Final state — complete, immutable, released |
| **The Queue** | The daily work surface — you don't choose, you serve the next thing |
| **Cue** | The button / interaction state — lightness in action |

**"I don't choose today. I work the Queue."**

---

### Item State Progression

```
QUEUED  →  CLASSIFIED  →  ROUTED  →  (REVIEWED)  →  WORKING  →  SEALED
```

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **QUEUED** | In INBOX, waiting for classification |
| **CLASSIFIED** | Label assigned, confidence scored |
| **ROUTED** | Moved to vessel folder or Review Queue |
| **REVIEWED** | Override applied — human judgment final |
| **WORKING** | Actively being developed in its form |
| **SEALED** | Complete, immutable, released |

### The Three Screens

**Screen 1 — The Queue**
Shows INBOX count. One button: **Run Jurisdiction**. The pipeline runs — classify, score, route. High confidence items route to vessels. Low confidence items route to Review.

**Screen 2 — The Review Screen**
One chunk at a time. Claude's suggested label and reasoning on the right. Six keys at the bottom.

| Key | Vessel |
|---|---|
| `P` | POEM |
| `S` | SONG |
| `O` | stORy |
| `F` | FRAMEWORK |
| `M` | MOMENT (hold — not ready) |

Press one key. The chunk moves. The override is logged. The next item loads.

**Screen 3 — The Corpus View**
Six vessel columns. Count and recent items per vessel. Items in Review waiting. This is a mirror, not a workbench.

## XVIII. TECHNICAL EXECUTION

### Stack

- **Runtime:** Node.js + Next.js (local, `localhost:3000`)
- **Storage:** Obsidian vault — plain `.md` files, no integration layer required
- **Classification:** Claude API (`claude-sonnet-4-6`)
- **No cloud services. No subscriptions. No automation chains.**

The app reads from and writes to your Obsidian vault directly. Obsidian sees everything natively.

---

### Vault Folder Structure

```
/INBOX/                     ← drop raw .md files here
/VESSELS/
    /POEM/
    /SONG/
    /STORY/
    /FRAMEWORK/
    /MOMENT/
    /ARCHIVE/
/REVIEW/                    ← low-confidence items awaiting override
/LOG/
    classifications.md      ← The Receipt — audit trail
    overrides.md            ← The Fix Button — correction record
```

---

### The Chunker

Splits `.md` files into discrete items using split signals in priority order:

| Priority | Signal |
|---|---|
| 1 | `---` explicit separator |
| 2 | Heading markers (`##`, `###`) |
| 3 | Two or more consecutive blank lines |
| 4 | Single blank line (if chunk is above minimum length) |
| 5 | 1200-character ceiling (hard cap) |

Chunk floor: 80 characters — below this, attach to adjacent chunk.

---

### Confidence Thresholds

| Confidence | Action |
|---|---|
| 0.9+ | Unmistakable. Routes directly to vessel. |
| 0.7–0.9 | Clear with minor uncertainty. Routes to vessel. |
| 0.5–0.7 | Genuinely ambiguous. Routes to REVIEW. |
| Below 0.5 | Assigns MOMENT. Never forces a form on unclear material. |

**Default threshold:** 0.65 — adjustable in config.

---

| Session | Goal | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| **0 — Ground Truth** | Manually label 25 representative chunks from the Song Idea Bank | The test set — calibration baseline before any code |

### Session 0 — Ground Truth (No Code)

Before writing a single line of the application:

Open the Song Idea Bank. Pull 25 representative chunks — pieces already known:

- 6 clearly songs
- 4 clearly poems
- 3 frameworks
- 3 genuine moments (unresolved, pre-form)
- 3 story seeds
- 3 that should be archived
- 3 genuinely ambiguous (the hard cases)

Label them manually using the rubric. Write one sentence for each: why this form. These 25 chunks are the **ground truth test set**. The classifier must get the unambiguous cases right before the full bank is touched. The ambiguous cases inform where the confidence threshold is set.

**This session has no code. It is the most important session.**

## XIX. THE CLASSIFICATION PROMPT

This is the rubric built from the distinctions in the source documents. It lives in `config/rubric.ts` and is sent with every chunk.

```
You are a craft classifier for a creative writing corpus.

Your job: read a chunk of text and assign it exactly ONE label.
Valid labels: POEM, SONG, STORY, FRAMEWORK, MOMENT, ARCHIVE

Return ONLY valid JSON — no explanation, no markdown, no preamble:
{
  "label": "SONG",
  "confidence": 0.82,
  "reasons": "Captures a state not an arc. Repetition would deepen. No explanation required.",
  "miscast_risk": "Could be POEM if melody removed — check line density",
  "excerpt": "first 80 characters of the chunk"
}

---

VESSEL DEFINITIONS:

POEM — Language that bears the weight by itself. Compressed meaning under pressure.
Must stand naked on the page. Survive silence. Justify every word. Feels like a verdict.
Repetition weakens it. Removing sound increases its power. Hits all at once, cannot
be repeated without loss. Said ONCE.

SONG — Language that asks to be carried. Meaning distributed across time.
Captures a state, not an arc. Holds tension without resolving it. Survives repetition
without context. Can be entered at any point. Grows stronger the second or tenth time.
Sound finishes what words cannot. Refuses explanation. Lived inside REPEATEDLY.
Rule: if the moment can live without "before" or "after" — song-eligible.

STORY — A sequence where choice creates consequence.
Requires a before/after boundary. A cost that cannot be undone.
A shift in who the person is allowed to be afterward.
If nothing is lost or irreversible — it is not a story. It is memory.
Rule: if the moment changes who you are allowed to be afterward — story-eligible.

FRAMEWORK — Meaning that lives beyond individuality.
Abstracts without erasing pain. Turns private insight into shared orientation.
A stranger can use it to make a decision or locate themselves without knowing the author.
Rule: if a moment can help someone decide — framework-eligible.

MOMENT — Raw, pre-craft. Lived, felt, charged, unordered.
Has not yet been asked what it demands. Too early to classify, or genuinely ambiguous.
MOMENT is the honest default — not a failure state.

ARCHIVE — Already did its work. Does not need to be transmitted.
Keeping it alive is sentimental, not faithful.
Archiving is not betrayal. It's respect.

---

FOUR DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS:

1. Does this collapse or strengthen when repeated?
   collapses → POEM or STORY / strengthens → SONG

2. Does removing context help or hurt?
   helps (stronger without background) → POEM
   hurts (needs context) → STORY or FRAMEWORK

3. Does this require judgment — verdict, cost, consequence?
   yes → STORY or FRAMEWORK / no (state, image) → POEM or SONG

4. Can someone use this without knowing the author?
   yes → FRAMEWORK / no → POEM / SONG / STORY

TIEBREAKER: Said once or lived inside repeatedly?
once → POEM / repeatedly → SONG

---

MISCAST SIGNALS — flag in miscast_risk if present:
- Feels wordy / melody would be scaffolding → POEM miscast as SONG
- Feels precious but inert / wants breath → SONG miscast as POEM
- Bogs in anecdote / meaning clearer summarized → FRAMEWORK miscast as STORY
- Feels bloodless / people ask "what happened?" → STORY miscast as FRAMEWORK

---

CHUNK TO CLASSIFY:
{CHUNK_TEXT}
```

## XX. NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES

These are the structural constraints that make the pipeline work. They are not suggestions.

---

| Rule | Statement |
|---|---|
| **One tag only** | Every piece gets ONE tag. No hybrids. No "for now." If it fights the tag — that's diagnostic, not a problem. |
| **Once assigned, other forms are banned** | A piece assigned to SONG is worked as a song. The ban is the point. Form restriction is where craft begins. |
| **Poems are source, not destination** | Every poem: SOURCE→SONG, SOURCE→STORY, or COMPLETE (sealed). No poems linger as "maybe." |
| **Frameworks must point outward** | Every framework answers: "Which song or story would this help finish?" If it can't — it's frozen. |
| **Finite containers** | Songs: 12 attempts per year. Stories: 3–5. Scarcity forces embodiment. |
| **Do not build UI first** | UI is Phase 2. The rubric is the thing. |
| **Research is warranted only** | "I am researching ___ in order to finish ___." Cannot fill both blanks = not allowed to research. |
| **Do not run the full corpus until the rubric is calibrated** | Ground truth session is not optional. |
| **Archive without guilt** | Archiving is completion in the correct direction, not failure. |
| **Seal without revision** | A sealed piece does not re-enter the pipeline. |
