## 1. WHY THIS EXISTS — THE REAL PROBLEM

### The Song Idea Bank

The Song Idea Bank is hundreds of pages of Obsidian markdown files. It contains songs, poems, story seeds, frameworks, moments, and archive debris — all undifferentiated, all alive, all unresolved. It is not a folder problem. It is a jurisdiction problem.

Material accumulates without being sentenced to a form when no system has authority over it. When every piece of writing can still be anything — can still become a song or a poem or a story — it stays in **permanent pre-form.** Nothing takes shape of the vessel. Nothing is finished. Stacks multiply.

The cost of this is not just organizational. It is creative and temporal. Hundreds of pages of material of unfinished artifacts and **high-cognitive-load configuration.** The loop feeds itself.

### The Pattern Behind the Problem

The stacks exist because the person building this system:

- notices constantly
- records faithfully
- does not discard prematurely
- treats experience as material

These are not failures. They are the correct behaviors of a serious creative practitioner. The problem is not capture — it is what happens after capture. Nothing after capture has had enforcement. No system has been authorized to say: *this goes here, and other forms are now banned.*

The deeper pattern: poems and frameworks have been the dominant output because they arrive without requiring commitment to time or to others. A poem can be written in one burst of compression. A framework can clarify endlessly. Neither forces closure. Neither demands imperfection. Neither binds the writer to consequence.

Songs and stories do. Songs require trust in embodiment — that sound and repetition can carry what words alone cannot. Stories require acceptance of consequence — that something must be lost or irreversible for it to count as story at all. The absence of finished songs and stories is not incapacity. It is pre-authorial restraint that has reached its expiration date.

### What Changes

Corpus Queue is the system that assigns jurisdiction.

Once a piece of material has a label, the other forms are banned. The mind calms. Cognitive load is reduced. The stack stops growing sideways and starts moving forward. The relief that comes from correct assignment is not aesthetic satisfaction — it is nervous system recognition that a loop has been closed.

This tool exists to close those loops at scale, across hundreds of pages of existing material, and to enforce that closure going forward.

## 2. THE PHILOSOPHICAL FOUNDATION

These principles come directly from the work already done in the execution docs. They are not theory — they are operational constraints that the tool is built to enforce.

### Form is assigned, not discovered.

Waiting to see what a piece "wants to become" is avoidance. Form is not discovered through observation. It is assigned through judgment. The moment you assign a form, other forms become unavailable. That restriction is not loss — it is the beginning of craft.

### Confusion about form is not uncertainty about meaning — it is lack of enforcement.

When a piece of writing seems like it could be a song or a poem or a story, that is not a sign that it contains multiple truths. It is a sign that no one has yet exercised authority over it. Corpus Queue is the enforcement mechanism.

### Everything stays alive when there is no jurisdiction. Execution kills.

This is the central law. The pipeline works because it kills possibility in the right direction. Killing a piece of writing's ability to be anything is not destruction — it is assignment. The work survives. Its optionality ends.

### Archive is not betrayal. It is respect.

Some material mattered. It shaped you. It does not need to be transmitted. Keeping it alive as a candidate for song or story when it has already done its work is sentimental, not faithful. Archiving is how you honor what something actually was without forcing it to be something it isn't.

### The real pipeline is: Moment → Form → Finish → Rest

Research, learning, and framework-building are not parallel tracks. They are fuel with a burn rate. They exist in service of finishing artifacts. Learning expands possibility. Craft requires contraction. This tool operates in a contraction phase.

### Poems and frameworks are source material, not destinations.

Poetry is the nervous system's native discharge. Judgment without deliberation. Truth without scaffolding. This is why it cannot be the flagship container — not because it is weak, but because it is too primary. It is what feeds everything else.

Frameworks clarify endlessly, which means they can justify infinite delay. A framework is done when it lets you make a decision, forces a cut, removes ambiguity, ends a question. If it opens more questions than it closes, it gets frozen.

Both poems and frameworks now serve song and story — or they rest.

## 3. THE SIX VESSELS — FULL DEFINITIONS

These definitions are the intellectual core of the tool. The classification prompt is built from them. They are not aesthetic preferences — they are craft obligations. Each form answers a different obligation: to time, to meaning, to audience, to repetition, to irreversibility.

---

### MOMENT
**What it is:** Raw, pre-craft material. Lived, felt, charged, unordered. A moment has not yet been asked what it demands. It waits.

**What it is not:** A form. MOMENT is not a destination — it is a holding state. Anything that has not yet been sentenced to a form is a MOMENT by default.

**When to assign MOMENT:** When the material is too early to classify, or when the classifier's confidence is below threshold. MOMENT is the honest default. It is not failure — it is accurate staging.

**Craft obligation:** None yet. Moments are sacred and dumb. They do not advance themselves.

**In the pipeline:** MOMENT → First Question → Form Assignment.

---

### POEM
**What it is:** Language that bears the weight entirely by itself. Compressed meaning under pressure. A poem must stand naked on the page, survive silence before and after, justify every word, and tolerate being misread without collapsing.

**Core demand:** Exact language. One landing. No rescue. A poem hits all at once and cannot be repeated without loss. It often breaks if you try to sing it. It does not want melody to rescue it. It does not want repetition to stabilize it. It does not want time to soften it.

**Key tests:**
- If removing sound *increases* its power → poem
- If repetition *weakens* it rather than strengthening it → poem
- If it feels like a verdict → poem
- If one wrong word ruins the whole thing → poem

**Relationship to song:** A poem is judgment. A song is mercy. Poems resolve internally — a poem finishes inside itself. Poems collapse optionality faster than any other form.

**Fear diagnostic:** When a piece scares you because it feels *too exact* — make it a poem. Poems are where you stop protecting the moment.

**Trap to avoid:** Treating a poem as lyrics without music. If it wants music, don't force it to stand.

**Craft obligation:** Must survive silence. Must justify every word. No melody, no backstory allowed in the working process.

**State progression:** POEM → working → SOURCE→SONG / SOURCE→STORY / COMPLETE (sealed).

---

### SONG
**What it is:** Language that asks to be carried. Meaning distributed across time. A song expects repetition, breath, return, and resonance rather than precision.

**Core demand:** Embodiment. No explanation, no moralizing, no arc, no apology. Sound, image, pressure. A song captures a state, not an arc. It holds tension without resolving it. It survives repetition without context. It can be entered at any point.

**Key tests:**
- If repetition *deepens* it rather than diluting it → song
- If removing context *helps* → might be poem; if removing context *hurts* → song needs its container
- If the moment can live without "before" or "after" → song-eligible
- If you can't imagine singing it without explaining it first → not a song. Explanation poisons songs.
- If sound can finish what words cannot → song

**Relationship to poem:** A song is mercy. Songs resolve relationally — a song finishes between performer and listener. Songs let you offload weight to sound, let silence carry meaning, trust embodiment. Songs grant mercy to the moment: they let it breathe, they let it repeat, they let others enter.

**Relationship to story:** A song is *not* a story. A song is a moment that refuses explanation. If a moment still needs background, causality, justification, or moral framing — it is pre-song material.

**Fear diagnostic:** When a piece scares you because it feels *too exposed* — make it a song. Songs are where you let it live.

**Trap to avoid:** Treating a song as a poem with chords. Songs don't want density. They want pressure and release.

**Craft obligation:** Must survive being sung imperfectly. Must allow listeners to inhabit it. Must repeat without boredom. Must leave space for the unsaid. Repetition allowed. Density discouraged.

**Limits (enforcement rule):** Maximum 12 song attempts per year. Scarcity forces embodiment.

---

### STORY
**What it is:** A sequence where choice creates consequence. Story requires a shift in orientation, a before/after boundary, and a cost that cannot be undone.

**Core demand:** Reckoning. Sequence, consequence, decision, cost. If nothing is lost or irreversible — it is not a story. It is memory.

**Key tests:**
- If the moment *changes who you are allowed to be afterward* → story-eligible
- If there is a before/after boundary that cannot be crossed back → story
- If something is lost that cannot be recovered → story
- If it requires judgment and a verdict over time → story

**Relationship to song:** A story needs arc. A song refuses arc. If the material insists on time and judgment — it belongs to story.

**Relationship to framework:** A story miscast as a framework becomes bloodless. People ask "but what happened?" Stakes feel missing.

**Trap to avoid:** A framework miscast as a story bogs down in anecdote. Meaning becomes clearer when summarized.

**Craft obligation:** Sequence and consequence required. Must survive causality. Ban lyricism in the working process.

**Limits (enforcement rule):** Maximum 3–5 story attempts per year.

---

### FRAMEWORK
**What it is:** Meaning that lives beyond individuality. A framework abstracts without erasing pain, generalizes without flattening truth, and turns private insight into shared orientation.

**Core demand:** Orientation. Pattern, distinction, naming, guidance. If a moment can help someone decide — it is framework-eligible.

**Key tests:**
- If someone can use this without knowing you → framework
- If it helps a stranger locate themselves → framework
- If it answers: "Which song or story would this help finish?" → active framework. If not → freeze it.

**Relationship to poem and story:** Frameworks are not allowed to advance themselves anymore. Every framework must point outward to a concrete artifact it helps finish.

**When a framework is done:** When it lets you make a decision. When it forces a cut. When it removes ambiguity. When it ends a question. If it opens more questions than it closes — it is frozen.

**Craft obligation:** Must survive use by strangers. Ban autobiography in the working process. No autonomous growth.

---

### ARCHIVE
**What it is:** Material that mattered, shaped you, but does not need to be transmitted. It already did its work on you.

**Core demand:** Nothing. This is important — archive demands nothing further.

**Key tests:**
- You keep it alive out of loyalty, not because it demands craft
- It doesn't ask to be finished
- It already resolved on you
- Archiving is not betrayal. It is respect.

**Craft obligation:** Must survive neglect. Zero guilt. Zero edits. Archiving is a completed action, not a holding state.

## 4. THE DIAGNOSTIC RUBRIC — FOUR QUESTIONS

These four questions are the classification engine. No debate, no aesthetics. Where the material lands most consistently across all four questions — that is the correct form.

---

**Question 1: Does this collapse or strengthen when repeated?**

- Collapses → POEM or STORY
- Strengthens → SONG

---

**Question 2: Does removing context help or hurt?**

- Helps (the piece gets stronger without background) → POEM
- Hurts (the piece needs its context to make sense) → STORY or FRAMEWORK

---

**Question 3: Does this require judgment?**

- Yes (there is a verdict, a cost, a consequence) → STORY or FRAMEWORK
- No (it is a state, an image, a compressed truth) → POEM or SONG

---

**Question 4: Can someone use this without knowing you?**

- Yes (it orients, it guides, it applies beyond autobiography) → FRAMEWORK
- No (it requires your specific life, your specific voice) → POEM / SONG / STORY

---

**Tiebreaker — the single most important question:**

**Does this want to be said once, or lived inside repeatedly?**

- Said once → POEM
- Lived inside → SONG
- If you can't imagine singing it without explaining it first → not a song

## 5. MISCAST MATERIAL — SYMPTOMS AND CORRECTIONS

Miscasting produces specific symptoms. These symptoms are not aesthetic preferences — they are diagnostic signals. The classifier uses these to flag potential miscasts for human review.

---

**A poem miscast as a song:**
- Feels wordy
- Melody feels like scaffolding, not carrier
- Repetition dulls rather than deepens it
- You keep "fixing the lyrics" without progress
- **Correction:** Strip sound. It is a poem.

---

**A song miscast as a poem:**
- Feels precious but inert
- Wants breath, wants a body
- Lines feel cramped on the page
- Silence kills it rather than framing it
- **Correction:** Add repetition and melody. It is a song.

---

**A framework miscast as a story:**
- Bogs down in anecdote
- Meaning is clearer when summarized
- Reader wants "the takeaway" rather than the scene
- **Correction:** Abstract it. It is a framework.

---

**A story miscast as a framework:**
- Feels bloodless
- People ask "but what happened?"
- Stakes feel missing
- **Correction:** Restore consequence. It is a story.

---

**Anything miscast when it should be archived:**
- You keep it alive out of loyalty
- It doesn't demand craft
- It already did its work on you
- **Correction:** Archive without guilt.

## 6. THE CANONICAL PIPELINE

This is the spine of the entire system. Every piece of material moves through these stages — or it doesn't move at all.

```
MOMENT (raw capture, pre-craft)
    ↓
FIRST QUESTION: "What does this demand from me?"
    (Not what it could be. What it demands. Demand = obligation.)
    ↓
FORM ASSIGNMENT (one tag, other forms banned)
    ↓
CRAFT DISCIPLINE (form-specific rules enforced)
    ↓
FINISH
    ↓
SEAL (complete, immutable, released)
    ↓
REST
```

---

### Stage 0 — MOMENT

A moment is lived, felt, charged, unordered. Rule: Moments do nothing yet. They wait. Capture is sacred and dumb — no thinking, no sorting at capture time. This is the one behavior the system requires from you.

---

### Stage 1 — FIRST QUESTION (Jurisdiction)

"What does this moment demand from me?"

Not: what could it be.
Not: what would be beautiful.
Not: what fits my current project.

Demand = obligation, not preference. This question is where the classifier operates.

---

### Stage 2 — FORM ASSIGNMENT

| If the moment demands | It becomes |
|---|---|
| Exact language, one landing, no rescue | POEM |
| Repetition, embodiment, shared inhabiting | SONG |
| Consequence, sequence, moral cost | STORY |
| Guidance, distinction, decision-making for others | FRAMEWORK |
| Nothing further | ARCHIVE |
| Unclear / too early | MOMENT (hold) |

Form is assigned, not discovered. Once assigned, other forms are banned. This is where discipline enters.

---

### Stage 3 — CRAFT DISCIPLINE

Each form has a rule it must obey. If a piece fails its rule after assignment, it is miscast — the tag changes, not the rule.

| Form | Rule it must obey |
|---|---|
| POEM | Must survive silence |
| SONG | Must survive repetition |
| STORY | Must survive causality |
| FRAMEWORK | Must survive use by strangers |
| ARCHIVE | Must survive neglect |

---

### Stage 4 — FINISH

Not perfection. Finish. The difference between a poem that is done and a poem that is perfect is that the first one exists and the second one doesn't.

---

### Stage 5 — SEAL

A sealed piece is complete and immutable. It does not re-enter the pipeline. It is released — into the world, into the corpus, into the record. Sealing is an act of authorship, not just organization.

---

### Stage 6 — REST

After sealing, the piece rests. You rest. Nothing is immediately reopened for revision. The work is complete.

---

## 7. CRAFT RULES — WHAT EACH FORM IS ALLOWED TO DO

These are the in-lane rules. Once a piece is assigned to a form, these rules govern how it is worked. Breaking a rule is diagnostic — it means wrong form, not wrong execution.

**POEM:**
- No melody in the working process
- No backstory, no setup
- Exact language only — one wrong word = start over
- Repetition is suspect — if you reach for it, question the form
- No moralizing, no explaining

**SONG:**
- Repetition is allowed and expected
- Density is discouraged — pressure and release, not weight
- No explanation in the lyric itself
- Ambiguity is fine — songs can circle instead of conclude
- Trust sound to finish what words start

**STORY:**
- Sequence and consequence required
- Ban lyricism — compression for its own sake derails story
- Something must be lost or irreversible
- No frameworks in the draft — no takeaways, no lessons, no orientation guides
- Character must make choices that cost something

**FRAMEWORK:**
- No autobiography — personal experience is evidence, not subject
- Must answer: "Which song or story does this help finish?"
- If it cannot answer that question → freeze it
- When it opens more questions than it closes → freeze it
- A framework is done when it forces a decision, not when it covers everything

**ARCHIVE:**
- Zero edits
- Zero guilt
- The work is done

## 8. THE TOOL — WHAT CORPUS QUEUE ACTUALLY IS

### In one sentence:

A local web application that reads your Obsidian markdown files, sends each chunk to Claude's API with your rubric as the classification prompt, assigns a vessel label with a confidence score, routes high-confidence items to vessel folders Obsidian can read natively, and holds low-confidence items in a Review Queue for your single-keystroke override.

### What it is not:

- A cathedral. It is not a system for everything.
- A Notion database. It does not require a cloud service.
- A Slack automation. It does not require an always-on workflow.
- A second brain for general productivity. It is a specific instrument for a specific creative problem.
- A UI-first project. The UI exists to serve the triage, not the other way around.

### What it replaces:

The existing situation: hundreds of pages of Song Idea Bank material sitting in Obsidian, undifferentiated, unrouted, alive in every direction and therefore unfinished in any direction.

### What it produces:

- Vessel folders inside your Obsidian vault, each containing only the material that belongs to that form
- A review queue for items requiring your judgment
- An audit log of every classification decision
- An override record that accumulates your corrections and teaches the system your voice over time

### The user experience in one paragraph:

You open the app at `localhost:3000`. The Queue screen shows how many items are in your Obsidian INBOX folder waiting to be classified. You hit "Run Jurisdiction." The app reads each file, chunks it, sends each chunk to Claude with your rubric, and routes everything with confidence above the threshold to its vessel folder. Items below threshold land in the Review screen. You go to Review. One chunk at a time. Claude's suggested label and its reasoning are visible on the right. You press one key — P, S, O, T, F, M, or A — and the chunk moves. You work through the queue. The Bank gets sorted.

## 9. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION

### Runtime Environment

**Local Next.js application** running at `localhost:3000`. No cloud hosting required. No subscription services. No third-party automation tools. The application reads from and writes to your local filesystem directly.

Node.js 20+ required.

### Storage Architecture

All data lives in your Obsidian vault as plain `.md` files. This means:
- Obsidian reads everything natively — no sync issues, no integration layer
- Every output is a real markdown file you can open, read, edit, and link from anywhere in your vault
- No proprietary database. No lock-in. If the app disappears, your files remain exactly where they are.

### Folder Structure (inside your Obsidian vault)

```
/INBOX/                    ← drop raw .md files here for processing
/VESSELS/
    /POEM/                 ← classified and routed poem material
    /SONG/                 ← classified and routed song material
    /STORY/                ← classified and routed story material
    /FRAMEWORK/            ← classified and routed framework material
    /MOMENT/               ← uncertain items / honest holds
    /ARCHIVE/              ← archived material
/REVIEW/                   ← low-confidence items awaiting your override
/LOG/
    classifications.md     ← audit trail: every decision logged
    overrides.md           ← your corrections: predicted vs. actual label
```

### Application File Structure

```
/corpus-queue/
├── app/
│   ├── page.tsx               ← Queue screen (main dashboard)
│   ├── review/page.tsx        ← Review screen (one chunk at a time)
│   ├── corpus/page.tsx        ← Corpus view (vessel counts + recent items)
│   └── api/
│       ├── classify/route.ts  ← calls Claude API, returns label + confidence
│       ├── route/route.ts     ← moves files to vessel folders
│       └── override/route.ts ← writes corrections to overrides.md
├── lib/
│   ├── chunker.ts             ← splits .md files into discrete chunks
│   ├── classifier.ts          ← builds prompt, calls API, parses response
│   ├── router.ts              ← file system operations (move, copy, append)
│   └── logger.ts              ← writes to classifications.md and overrides.md
├── config/
│   └── rubric.ts              ← your classification prompt lives here
└── .env.local                 ← ANTHROPIC_API_KEY, VAULT_PATH
```

### The Chunker

The chunker is the first operation on every file. It takes a `.md` file and splits it into discrete items using a priority-ordered set of split signals:

**Split signals in priority order:**
1. `---` (explicit separator — highest confidence boundary)
2. Heading markers (`##`, `###`)
3. Two or more consecutive blank lines
4. Single blank line (if chunk is above minimum length)
5. Character ceiling: 1200 characters (hard cap — never exceed)

**Chunk floor:** 80 characters. Anything below is attached to the adjacent chunk rather than classified independently.

Each chunk is assigned a unique `chunk_id` derived from: `filename-chunkindex-hash`. This ID is stable across reruns and is used to match corrections in `overrides.md` back to their source.

### The Classifier

The classifier sends each chunk to Claude's API (model: `claude-sonnet-4-6`) with:

1. The system prompt containing your rubric (lives in `config/rubric.ts`)
2. The chunk text
3. An instruction to return structured JSON only

**Required JSON response format:**
```json
{
  "label": "SONG",
  "confidence": 0.82,
  "reasons": "Captures a state without arc. Repetition would deepen. No explanation required.",
  "miscast_risk": "Could be POEM if melody removed — check line density",
  "excerpt": "first 100 characters of chunk"
}
```

**Valid labels:** `POEM`, `SONG`, `STORY`, `FRAMEWORK`, `MOMENT`, `ARCHIVE`

**Confidence threshold:** 0.65 default (adjustable in config). Items below threshold → REVIEW folder, not vessel folder.

**Cost estimate:** At ~500 characters per chunk, classification calls cost approximately $0.001–0.003 each. A 400-page Song Idea Bank chunked into ~800 items costs under $3 to classify in full.

### The Router

After classification, the router:

1. **High confidence (≥ 0.65):** Moves chunk to `/VESSELS/{LABEL}/{original_filename}_{chunk_id}.md`
2. **Low confidence (< 0.65):** Moves chunk to `/REVIEW/{original_filename}_{chunk_id}.md` with classification metadata prepended as YAML frontmatter
3. **Logs every decision** to `/LOG/classifications.md`

File format in vessel folders:

```markdown
---
source: SONG_IDEA_BANK_2024.md
chunk_id: song-idea-bank-0042-a8f3
label: SONG
confidence: 0.87
classified: 2025-02-21
reasons: Captures state not arc. Repetition would deepen. No explanation required.
---

[chunk text here]
```

### The Review Screen

**Layout:**
- Center panel: full chunk text
- Right panel: Claude's suggested label, confidence score, reasoning, miscast risk note
- Bottom bar: six large keyboard shortcut buttons

**Keyboard shortcuts:**
- `P` → POEM
- `S` → SONG
- `O` → stOry (T conflicts; O for stOry, or configure to your preference)
- `F` → FRAMEWORK
- `M` → MOMENT (hold — not ready)
- `A` → ARCHIVE

On keypress: file moves to correct vessel folder, override is logged to `overrides.md`, next item loads automatically.

**Override log format:**
```
chunk_id: song-idea-bank-0042-a8f3
predicted: POEM
corrected: SONG
date: 2025-02-21
```

### The Corpus View

Three-column layout. One column per primary active vessel (POEM, SONG, STORY). Shows:
- Total item count
- Last 5 items added (title/excerpt)
- Items in REVIEW waiting

This is not a workbench. It is a mirror. You see what is accumulating without being invited to work on it from this screen.

### The API Key

Set in `.env.local`:
```
ANTHROPIC_API_KEY=your_key_here
VAULT_PATH=/Users/yourname/path/to/obsidian/vault
```

The app never sends your material anywhere except the Anthropic API for classification. Your files stay on your machine.

## 10. BUILD SEQUENCE — SESSION BY SESSION

### Before Any Code: Ground Truth Session

Before writing a single line of the application, do this:

Open the Song Idea Bank. Manually pull 25 representative chunks — pieces you already know the answer to:
- 6 that are clearly songs
- 4 that are clearly poems
- 3 that are frameworks
- 3 that are genuine moments (unresolved, pre-form)
- 3 that are story seeds
- 3 that should be archived
- 3 that are genuinely ambiguous (the hard cases)

Label them manually using the rubric in Section 4. Write one sentence for each explaining why. These 25 labeled chunks are your ground truth test set. You will run the classifier against them before processing the full bank. The classifier must get the unambiguous cases right. The ambiguous cases will inform where the confidence threshold needs to be set.

This session has no code. It is the most important session.

---

### Session 1 — Vault Prep and Chunker (1–2 hours)

**Goal:** Take the Song Idea Bank from its current state to a folder of discrete numbered chunks in `/INBOX/`.

1. Consolidate your Song Idea Bank files into one parent folder if they aren't already
2. Set up the project: `npx create-next-app corpus-queue`, configure `.env.local`
3. Write `lib/chunker.ts` — reads all `.md` files from the vault path, applies split signals, outputs numbered chunks to `/INBOX/`
4. Run the chunker on a single test file (not the whole bank)
5. Manually inspect 20 output chunks — verify splits are landing on real item boundaries, not in the middle of pieces
6. Adjust split signal weights if needed
7. Run on full Song Idea Bank

**Deliverable:** `/INBOX/` folder containing discrete, numbered chunks. No classification yet. Just items.

---

### Session 2 — Classifier and Rubric Calibration (2–3 hours)

**Goal:** Build the API call and validate the rubric against your ground truth set.

1. Write `config/rubric.ts` with the full classification prompt (see Section 11)
2. Write `lib/classifier.ts` — builds prompt, calls `claude-sonnet-4-6`, parses JSON response
3. Write a test script that runs the classifier against your 25 ground truth chunks only
4. Review results: where did it get the unambiguous cases right? Where did it fail?
5. Adjust rubric language based on failures — this is prompt engineering, not code changes
6. Run again. Target: 90%+ accuracy on unambiguous cases before touching the full bank
7. Note which cases landed in ambiguous territory — set confidence threshold accordingly

**Deliverable:** A calibrated rubric that correctly handles your specific material. A validated classifier.

---

### Session 3 — Router and Full Bank Run (1–2 hours)

**Goal:** Run the full classification pipeline on the Song Idea Bank.

1. Write `lib/router.ts` — file move operations, YAML frontmatter injection
2. Write `lib/logger.ts` — writes to `classifications.md`
3. Run the full pipeline on all `/INBOX/` chunks
4. Check vessel folders — spot-check 20 items from each vessel
5. Check `/REVIEW/` folder — how many items landed here? (expect 15–25% of total)
6. Adjust confidence threshold if review queue is too large or too small

**Deliverable:** Classified, routed corpus. Vessel folders populated. Review queue populated.

---

### Session 4 — Review Screen (2–3 hours)

**Goal:** Build the interface for working through the review queue efficiently.

## 11. THE CLASSIFICATION PROMPT

This is the rubric built directly from the definitions in the execution docs. This prompt lives in `config/rubric.ts` and is sent to Claude with every chunk.

```
You are a craft classifier for a creative writing corpus.

Your job is to read a chunk of text and assign it exactly ONE label from this list:
POEM, SONG, STORY, FRAMEWORK, MOMENT, ARCHIVE

Return ONLY valid JSON in this exact format — no explanation, no markdown, no preamble:
{
  "label": "SONG",
  "confidence": 0.82,
  "reasons": "brief explanation of why this label, what signals pointed here",
  "miscast_risk": "note if there is a meaningful risk this is a different form",
  "excerpt": "first 80 characters of the chunk"
}

---

VESSEL DEFINITIONS:

POEM: Language that bears the weight entirely by itself. Compressed meaning under pressure.
A poem must stand naked on the page. Repetition weakens it. Sound would not help it —
removing sound increases its power. It feels like a verdict. One wrong word ruins it.
It hits all at once and cannot be repeated without loss.
Key signal: "Does this want to be said ONCE?"

SONG: Language that asks to be carried. Meaning distributed across time.
A song expects repetition, breath, return. It captures a STATE, not an arc.
It holds tension without resolving it. It can be entered at any point.
Repetition deepens it. Sound finishes what words start.
It does NOT need background, causality, justification, or moral framing to work.
If you can't imagine singing it without explaining it first — it is NOT a song.
Key signal: "Does this want to be LIVED INSIDE repeatedly?"

STORY: A sequence where choice creates consequence.
Requires: a before/after boundary, a cost that cannot be undone, a shift in who
the person is allowed to be afterward. If nothing is lost or irreversible — it is
not a story, it is memory.
Key signal: "Does something irreversible happen here?"

FRAMEWORK: Meaning that lives beyond individuality.
Abstracts without erasing pain. Turns private insight into shared orientation.
Someone who doesn't know the author can use it to make a decision or locate themselves.
Key signal: "Can a stranger use this without knowing me?"

MOMENT: Raw, pre-craft material. Lived, felt, charged, unordered.
Has not yet been asked what it demands. Too early to classify, OR genuinely ambiguous
across multiple forms. Use MOMENT when confidence is low — it is the honest default,
not a failure state.
Key signal: "Is this still waiting to be sentenced to a form?"

ARCHIVE: Material that mattered, shaped the author, but does not need to be transmitted.
It already did its work. Keeping it alive as a candidate is sentimental, not faithful.
Key signal: "Has this already done everything it's going to do?"

---

FOUR DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS — apply in order:

1. Does this collapse or strengthen when repeated?
   — collapses → POEM or STORY
   — strengthens → SONG

2. Does removing context help or hurt?
   — helps (stronger without background) → POEM
   — hurts (needs context to work) → STORY or FRAMEWORK

3. Does this require judgment — a verdict, a cost, a consequence?
   — yes → STORY or FRAMEWORK
   — no (it is a state, an image) → POEM or SONG

4. Can someone use this without knowing the author?
   — yes → FRAMEWORK
   — no → POEM / SONG / STORY

TIEBREAKER: Does this want to be said once, or lived inside repeatedly?
— said once → POEM
— lived inside → SONG

---

MISCAST SIGNALS — flag these in miscast_risk if present:

- Feels wordy / melody would be scaffolding → might be POEM miscast as SONG
- Feels precious but inert / wants breath → might be SONG miscast as POEM
- Bogs in anecdote / meaning clearer when summarized → might be FRAMEWORK miscast as STORY
- Feels bloodless / people would ask "but what happened?" → might be STORY miscast as FRAMEWORK

---

CONFIDENCE GUIDANCE:
0.9+ = unmistakable. Strong signals, single form, no ambiguity.
0.7–0.9 = clear with minor uncertainty. One form dominates.
0.5–0.7 = genuinely ambiguous. Flag for human review. Consider MOMENT.
Below 0.5 = assign MOMENT. Do not force a form on unclear material.

---

The chunk to classify:

{CHUNK_TEXT}
```

## 12. ECOSYSTEM AND STORAGE

### Obsidian as the Storage Layer

Your material lives in Obsidian vault folders as plain `.md` files. This is the correct architecture because:

- Obsidian reads the vessel folders natively with no integration required
- You can create Obsidian views, tags, and links on classified material immediately
- Obsidian's search works across all vessel content
- The files are portable — they will exist and be readable long after any application layer

**The separator principle from the Second Brain outline applies here:** the app (compute layer) and your vault (memory layer) are separate. The app can be rebuilt, updated, or replaced without touching your classified material. Your vault is the source of truth.

### What Obsidian Gives You After Classification

Once the vessel folders are populated:
- Create an Obsidian dashboard page that links to each vessel folder
- Use Dataview plugin to build dynamic views: "all SONG items added this week," "all POEM items marked SOURCE→STORY"
- Tag sealed items with `#sealed` — they no longer appear in active work views
- Tag items with `#working` when you are actively developing them

### What the App Gives You That Obsidian Does Not

- Automated classification of raw intake
- Confidence-scored routing — the bouncer that keeps garbage out of vessels
- The review queue — a discipline tool, not just a UI
- The override log — your editorial voice accumulating over time

### Obsidian vs. Notion for This Project

The video outline recommended Notion for automation pipelines because Notion has a clean API that tools like Zapier can write to reliably. For a cloud-automation workflow, this is correct.

For a local-first, personally-built application reading your own filesystem, Obsidian is correct. There is no syncing complexity because the app and the vault are on the same machine. The "plumbing frustration" the outline warned about does not apply here.

## 13. NAMING AND VOCABULARY

### The System Name: CORPUS QUEUE

**Corpus Queue** is the right name because:
- **Corpus** signals that there is a body of work — that not everything belongs to it, that entry is earned, that sequence determines survival
- **Queue** enforces order, patience, and humility — you don't choose in a queue, you serve the next thing
- Together they say: "This is where material becomes part of the body — one item at a time."

It is not a sandbox. It is not a playground. It is not a possibility space. It is a canon machine.

### Internal Vocabulary

**Jurisdiction** — the action the app takes when it assigns a label. "Run Jurisdiction" is the button that triggers the classification pipeline.

**Vessels** — the six destination forms. Material is routed *into* vessels. A vessel holds what belongs to it.

**Seal** — the final state of a completed piece. Sealed material is immutable. It does not re-enter the pipeline.

**Archive** — not a trash bin. A completed category. Archived material is respected, not discarded.

**MOMENT** — the honest default. Not a failure state. The accurate label for material that is not ready to be sentenced to a form.

### Item State Progression

```
QUEUED → CLASSIFIED → ROUTED → (REVIEWED) → WORKING → SEALED
```

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| QUEUED | In INBOX, waiting for classification |
| CLASSIFIED | Label assigned, confidence scored |
| ROUTED | Moved to vessel folder or review queue |
| REVIEWED | Override applied by human |
| WORKING | Actively being developed in its form |
| SEALED | Complete, immutable, released |

### The One Sentence That Tests the Name

*"I don't choose today. I work the Queue."*

If that lands — the name is correct.

## 14. THE OVERRIDE LOOP — HOW IT BECOMES YOUR INSTRUMENT

The override loop is the mechanism that turns Corpus Queue from a generic classifier into your specific instrument. It is not a correction mechanism — it is a teaching mechanism.

### How It Works

Every time you disagree with a classification in the review screen and press a different key, the app logs:

```
chunk_id: [stable hash]
predicted: POEM
corrected: SONG
date: 2025-02-21
note: [optional — you can add one]
```

This log accumulates in `overrides.md`.

### What You Do With It

After every 50 corrections, open `overrides.md` and look for patterns:

- **If POEM → SONG is frequent:** The rubric is misidentifying songs as poems. The distinction between "said once" vs. "lived inside repeatedly" needs sharper language or additional test cases in the prompt.
- **If MOMENT → SONG or MOMENT → POEM is frequent:** The confidence threshold is too low — the classifier is punting to MOMENT on material that is actually classifiable. Raise the threshold slightly.
- **If ARCHIVE → MOMENT is frequent:** You are overriding archive assignments back to MOMENT — which means you're not ready to archive that material yet. This is diagnostic about you, not the classifier.

Each round of rubric refinement after override review is one hour of work. The classifier improves with your voice over time. After 200–300 corrections, confidence scores become reliable enough that you will rarely need to use the review screen at all.

### The Instrument Principle

A classifier that is purely generic cannot serve your material. Your distinctions — between a song and a poem, between a moment and a story seed — are specific to your voice, your thresholds, your history with these forms. The override loop is how those specifics accumulate into the system. It is how the tool stops being an AI's interpretation of form and becomes an extension of your own editorial judgment.

## 15. RULES THAT CANNOT BE BROKEN

These are pulled directly from the execution docs. They are not suggestions. They are the structural constraints that make the pipeline work.

---

**Rule 1: Every piece gets ONE tag only.**

No hybrids. No "for now." No "it could be a song or a poem." If it fights the tag, that is diagnostic — not a problem with the tag. Fighting the tag means the piece is miscast or genuinely pre-form (MOMENT). One tag, enforced.

---

**Rule 2: Once assigned, other forms are banned.**

A piece assigned to SONG is worked as a song. You do not pursue it as a poem while also pursuing it as a song. The ban is the point. Form restriction is where craft begins.

---

**Rule 3: Poems and frameworks are no longer allowed to advance themselves.**

Every poem must be marked one of three things: SOURCE→SONG, SOURCE→STORY, or COMPLETE (sealed). No poems are allowed to linger as "maybe."

Every framework note must answer: "Which song or story would this help finish?" If it cannot answer that question, it is frozen.

---

**Rule 4: Songs — limit 12 attempts per year. Stories — limit 3–5 per year.**

Scarcity forces embodiment. You do not get to keep starting new ones. Constraint is not deprivation — it is the condition under which commitment becomes possible.

---

**Rule 5: Do not build UI first.**

UI is Phase 2. Phase 1 is the chunker, the classifier, and the router. A beautiful app that classifies incorrectly is worse than a script that classifies correctly. The rubric is the thing. The interface serves the rubric.

---

**Rule 6: Do not research unless a piece is already assigned to a form and blocked.**

Write the sentence before researching: *"I am researching ___ in order to finish ___."* If you cannot fill both blanks, you are not allowed to research. Research sessions are 45–90 minutes maximum, one question only, notes go directly into the assigned piece — not a general vault.

---

**Rule 7: Do not run the full corpus through the classifier until the rubric is calibrated on the ground truth set.**

The ground truth session (25 manually labeled chunks) is not optional. Running the full bank through an uncalibrated classifier produces high-volume garbage output that takes longer to correct than doing it right the first time.

---

**Rule 8: Archive without guilt.**

Archiving is not failure. It is not loss. It is completion in the correct direction. Material that has already done its work on you does not need to be transmitted. Keeping it alive as a candidate is a form of sentimentality that costs forward momentum.
